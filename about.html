Aranet4 ACH estimator v3
(source code)

In certain circumstances, CO2 in a room can be used as a 'tracer gas' in order to estimate 'air changes per hour' (ACH), a standard measure of ventilation effectiveness in the room. (For a nice explanation of this method, please see Dustin Poppendieck's Twitter thread on ACH estimation using CO2.)

For example: if a relatively air-tight room is full of people, and then everyone suddenly leaves for lunch, the CO2 level in the room is expected to decay exponentially; the associated decay rate, measured in hours, is the ACH in the room.
